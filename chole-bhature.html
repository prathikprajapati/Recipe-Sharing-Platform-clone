Chole Bhature

Serves: 4 people

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Chickpeas (Chole), soaked overnight
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1 large Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 Tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tsp Ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp Ground Coriander
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tsp Garam Masala
  • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
  • 1 tsp Amchur (Dry Mango Powder)
  • Salt to taste
  • 4 Bhature (Indian flatbread)
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, cook the soaked chickpeas with water until soft. Drain the excess water.
  2. Heat oil in a pan. Add chopped onions and saut√© until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until soft.
  4. Stir in the spices: coriander, cumin, garam masala, red chili powder, and amchur. Cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked chickpeas to the pan and mix well. Add salt and a little water to make a thick gravy. Let it simmer for 15-20 minutes.
  6. While the chole cooks, heat the Bhature on a griddle or in the oven.
  7. Serve the hot chole with warm kulche. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
